üìö About UPSC CSE

The Union Public Service Commission (UPSC) conducts the Civil Services Examination (CSE) annually to recruit candidates for prestigious Group 'A' and Group 'B' civil services of the Government of India, including the Indian Administrative Service (IAS), Indian Police Service (IPS), Indian Foreign Service (IFS), and many others. It's one of the most challenging yet rewarding exams in India.

üéØ Exam Stages:

  • ‚óè
    Preliminary Examination (Prelims): An objective-type, qualifying stage. It comprises two papers: General Studies Paper I and CSAT Paper II.
  • ‚óè
    Main Examination (Mains): A written descriptive examination, crucial for merit. It consists of nine papers, including an essay, four general studies papers, two optional subject papers, and two qualifying language papers.
  • ‚óè
    Personality Test (Interview): The final stage, assessing a candidate's mental aptitude, critical thinking, and suitability for a career in public service.

‚úÖ Eligibility Highlights:

  • ‚óè
    Age: Generally 21 to 32 years, with specific relaxations for reserved categories (OBC, SC/ST, PwBD).
  • ‚óè
    Education: A Bachelor's degree from a recognized university or equivalent qualification.
  • ‚óè
    Attempts: Varies by category (e.g., 6 for General, 9 for OBC, unlimited for SC/ST).

üìù UPSC CSE Syllabus

A thorough understanding of the detailed syllabus is the cornerstone of effective preparation. The syllabus is extensive, covering a wide array of subjects to test a candidate's comprehensive knowledge.

‚ú® Prelims Syllabus Overview:

  • ‚óè
    General Studies Paper I: Focuses on Current events of national & international importance, History of India & Indian National Movement, Indian & World Geography, Indian Polity & Governance (Constitution, Political System, Panchayati Raj, Public Policy, Rights Issues, etc.), Economic & Social Development (Sustainable Development, Poverty, Inclusion, Demographics, Social Sector Initiatives, etc.), Environmental Ecology, Bio-diversity & Climate Change, and General Science.
  • ‚óè
    CSAT Paper II: A qualifying paper testing Comprehension, Interpersonal skills including communication skills, Logical reasoning & analytical ability, Decision-making & problem-solving, General mental ability, and Basic numeracy (numbers and their relations, orders of magnitude, etc. - Class X level), Data interpretation (charts, graphs, tables, data sufficiency etc. - Class X level).

‚úçÔ∏è Mains Syllabus Overview:

The Mains examination comprises nine papers. Four General Studies Papers (GS I, II, III, IV) cover topics ranging from Indian Heritage & Culture, History & Geography of the World & Society, Governance, Constitution, Polity, Social Justice & International Relations, Technology, Economic Development, Bio-diversity, Environment, Security & Disaster Management, to Ethics, Integrity & Aptitude. Additionally, there's an Essay paper, two optional subject papers (chosen by the candidate), and two qualifying language papers (one Indian language and English).
